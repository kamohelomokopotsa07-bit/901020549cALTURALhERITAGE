MOHOBELO DANCE

CULTURAL DANCES

MOHOBELO

Mohobelo is a popular traditional dance performed by men, Mohobelo is known for its energetic and rhythmic movements, including high leaps and stomping feet.Mohobelo is mostly accompanied by strong body movements, rhythm, and singing, rather than modern instruments. The body itself becomes part of the music.

INTSUMENTS

  • Mokhope - a traditional percussion instrument made from wood or metal, played by tapping or striking to create rhythm.
  • Drum (Moropa) - sed to keep a steady beat and energize the dancers.
  • Sticks (Melamu) - sometimes used by dancers as part of the performance and rhyth
  • Hand clapping & foot stomping - these are also considered natural rhythmic instruments in traditional dances.
  • Whistle (Fereko) – Blown by the leader to direct the dance or signal changes.

LITOLOBONYA

Litolobonya is a traditional Sesotho form of song and dance exclusive to girls and married women, with the women's version performed secretly at women-only celebrations called pitiki, following a child's birth

INSTUMENTS

  • Sekupu - used to keep rhythm
  • Whistle - used to signal and add sharp sound effects during the dance.
  • Bottle caps - used for making jingling sounds as the dancer moves.
  • Skirts - often decorated to create sound while dancing (not a music instrument per se but used for rhythm).

MOKHIBO

Mokhibo is a traditional Basotho women's dance characterized by rhythmic, kneeling shoulder and neck movements, often performed at celebrations and festivals.

INSTUMENTS

  • Sekupo - is a drum-like instrument (traditionally made from animal skin). It provides rhythm and beats that guide the dance movements.
  • Hand Clapping – provides rhythm.
  • Singing – traditional songs guide the dance.
  • Drums - in some performances, small drums may accompany the dance.

SETEPO

A social dance with footwork and clapping, often performed at celebrations and gatherings.

INSTRUMENTS

  • Drums(Sekupu) - provide the main rhythm and energy for the dance.
  • Rattles hands for clapping - dancers often use rhythmic hand claps as part of the performance.
  • Rattles (Made from bottle caps or beads) – worn on ankles or held to enhance the beat.
  • Whistles (Phalafala) – used to add sharp sounds and signals during performance.
